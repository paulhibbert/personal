# Granada, Spain

## Overview

Granada is a vibrant city in southern Spain, located in the autonomous community of Andalusia. Situated at the foot of the Sierra Nevada mountains, it's known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and cultural heritage.

## History

Founded by the Romans, Granada later became the last stronghold of the Muslim Al-Andalus kingdom. The city flourished under Moorish rule until its conquest in 1492, marking the end of the Reconquista.

## Key Attractions


### The Alhambra

A magnificent palace and fortress complex featuring intricate Islamic architecture, stunning courtyards, and breathtaking views of the city and mountains.

### Cathedral and Royal Chapel

Built after the Reconquista, these impressive structures showcase Gothic and Renaissance architecture and contain the tombs of Catholic monarchs.

### Albaicín

The old Moorish quarter with narrow winding streets, whitewashed houses, and traditional shops and tapas bars.

## Culture & Food

Granada is famous for its tapas culture—enjoying free appetizers with drinks. The city offers excellent local cuisine, including jamón ibérico and fresh Mediterranean ingredients.

## Climate

Mediterranean climate with mild winters and warm summers. The nearby Sierra Nevada provides opportunities for winter sports.

## Getting Around

The historic center is walkable. The city has good public transportation and is well-connected by train and bus to other Spanish cities.
